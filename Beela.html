Background

In Phase 1, Tech Fleet set out to learn who makes up Beelaâ€™s community, what their journey into tech looks like, and why they join Beela. Here are some high-level findings from a survey distributed by Phase 1:

Who are they?

  • Most survey respondents are women
  • Most respondents are immigrants to Sweden
  • Most respondents are transitioning to tech, but there are also a few who are just curious or are experienced tech professionals
  • Most respondents are not employed
  • Respondents tend to be highly educated
  • Most respondents are married or in a long-term relationship, but do not have children

What does their immigration journey look like?

  • Most respondents move to Sweden with a partner.
  • On average, those who immigrated to Sweden have been there for two years.
  • On average, respondents rated their ability to speak Swedish as poor to adequate.
  • The key pain points experienced around immigration were bureaucratic challenges, career challenges, language and culture barriers, and lacking support.

What does their journey into tech look like?

  • The main reasons for transitioning to tech include personal fulfillment, career benefits, less
  • The key pain points experienced when transitioning to tech include getting started, shifting careers, lacking knowledge and mentorship, limited resources, self-doubt, and lack of belonging.

Why do they join Beela?

  • Most respondents heard about Beela via social media and referrals.
  • The main reasons for joining Beela include its mission and founders, as well as seeking new knowledge and a sense of social connection.
  • The key benefits respondents hope to gain from Beela include building community, gaining knowledge, and having the opportunity to help others.
  • In general, respondents are aware of Beelaâ€™s programmes, but for Pollination Mentoring and Beelaâ€™s Talk Podcast, fewer respondents actually use them.

Align

with client goals

In order to do the most effective and useful work possible, we began Phase 2 with an outcomes workshop with our client.

Why we did this: Focusing our efforts on one business goal allowed us to target our user-centered research and design efforts on activities that would support the most important business problem and focus on outcomes (increased revenue) rather than outputs (a new homepage).

What I did: Nonprofit revenue presentation

We met the Beela Stakeholders in Miro and asked them to individually create stickies with:

  1. Their priorities for Beela
  2. Their most important business outcomes

Next, we asked them to connect their priorities to the business outcome those priorities would drive.

Then, we did a quick grouping activities, labeled the groups, and asked them to prioritize the labeled groups in order of importance

The stakeholders prioritized REVENUE.

Revenue Models: As a result of this workshop, I researched and presented a handful of possible nonprofit revenue models for Beela to consider. Different models relied on support from individuals, companies, governments, and former benefactors to varying degrees. The unifying trait of the models? Results.

What We Learned: The workshop resulted in a clear understanding that we needed to help Beela generate revenue. This was a challenge because of Beelaâ€™s commitment to provide free services to users.

Beela would need to base their fundraising on Key Performance Indicators that spoke to their success, such as placement rates, conversion rates, number of members, duration of membership, etc.

Synthesize

Phase 1 Research

In addition to a survey, Phase 1 conducted several interviews. We affinity mapped the findings, identified pain points, and turned them into business opportunities. Once the data was organized, we asked stakeholders to prioritize the opportunities we presented.

Why we did this: To keep users front and center in our process, to have a structured framework to refer back to in later sprints, to communicate our work in a way that stakeholders would understand.

What I did: Individual Affinity Map, Collaborated on Team Affinity Map

Affinity Map: Each team member asynchronously affinity mapped the sticky notes from Phase 1. This allowed us to identify trends on our own before coming together to discuss. We all saw hot spots around finding community, getting support, and questions about how or where to start.

What We Learned: Users have various goals and pain points around starting a new career, but those goals overlapped at a high level.
